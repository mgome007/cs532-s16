<p>
I am a Bioinformatics Scientist at <a href="http://www.bina.com/" target="_blank">Bina Technologies</a>, part of <a href="http://sequencing.roche.com/" target="_blank">Roche Sequencing</a>. A little over 10 years ago, the Human Genome Project was complete after 10 years and $10 billion, which sequenced 3 people. It was a monumental achievement unthinkable only a few years prior. 
Today, we can sequence a genome for roughly $1000 in a single day. However, sequencing output outpaces Moore's Law, and data analysis and management has become the bottleneck of genomic studies.
Bina Technologies is solving the problem, in order to fully realize the potential of modern genomics and personalized medicine.
</p>

<p>
Check out <a href="http://bioinform.github.io/somaticseq/" target=_blank><b>SomaticSeq</b></a>, a machine learning algorithm we have developed at Bina to detect somatic mutations in cancer sequencing. 
SomaticSeq has placed Bina <a href="http://blog.bina.com/news/bina-places-1st-and-2nd-in-dream-challenge-establishing-potential-future-standards-for-cancer-genome-analysis" target=_blank>No. 1 in INDEL and No. 2 in SNV</a> in the Stage 5 of the <a href="https://www.synapse.org/#!Synapse:syn312572/wiki/70726" target="_blank">ICGC-TCGA DREAM Somatic Mutation Calling Challenge</a>.
Our </a><a href="http://dx.doi.org/10.1186/s13059-015-0758-2" target=_blank>paper</a> describing the method is an <a href="publications/BMC_Editors_pick.png">Editor's pick</a> in Genome Biology.
</p>




<p>
Check out my friend's start up company <a href="http://targetplex.com/" target="_blank">TargetPlex Genomics</a>. 
He has a new way to perform multiplex PCR, in order to amplify regions of interest in the genome for targeted deep sequencing. 
</p>

<div style="margin-left: 80px; margin-right: 80px">

My home server is powered by <a href="http://httpd.apache.org/" target="_blank">Apache HTTP Server</a>, 
running on 64-bit <a href="http://www.ubuntu.com/server" target="_blank">Ubuntu Linux Server Edition 14.04.3</a> on an <a href="http://ark.intel.com/products/49490" target="_blank">Intel Atom D525</a>. 
<br>

Why Intel Atom? Because its maximum power consumption is only 13 Watts. 
<br>

My home network is routed through a ASUS RT-N66R, with its factory firmware replaced by <a href="http://www.dd-wrt.com/" target="_blank">DD-WRT</a>. 
<!--enhanced with <a href="http://www.dd-wrt.com/phpBB2/viewtopic.php?p=693967" target="_blank">Optware The Right Way, Take 2</a>.--> 
<br>

However, my Linux Server is physically attached to a Netgear WNDR3700 v3, also with DD-WRT, set up as a <a href="http://www.dd-wrt.com/wiki/index.php/Repeater_Bridge" target="_blank">Wireless Repeater Bridge</a>.
<br>

If your feel this page loads too slowly, blame my <a href="http://sonic.net/" target="_blank">ISP</a>. They only gave me a measly 1.5 Mbps upload speed! Google Fiber, where are you!?<br>
