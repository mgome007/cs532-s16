    <header>
        <h1 class="p-name entry-title"><a href="posts/all-about-the-site.html" class="u-url">All about Lulzbot, Simplify3D, and 3D Printing</a></h1>
        <div class="metadata">
            <p class="byline author vcard"><span class="byline-name fn">Brian J. Geiger</span></p>
            <p class="dateline"><a href="posts/all-about-the-site.html" rel="bookmark"><time class="published dt-published" datetime="2015-03-05T17:00:00-05:00" itemprop="datePublished" title="Publication date">2015-03-05 17:00</time></a></p>
        </div>
    </header>
    <div class="e-content entry-content">
    <p>Welcome to Beep Boop Click, my new site for discussing some of my journey in 3D Printing. I've been working with some printers over the years, but I've just gotten a <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00S54E1AI/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00S54E1AI&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=egowaffle&amp;linkId=ZOLXXNLWNKQXXJ3J">LulzBot Mini</a><img src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=egowaffle&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00S54E1AI" width="1" height="1" border="0" alt="" style="border:none !important; margin:0px !important;">
 (Amazon Affiliate Link), which is their brand-new model. </p>
<p>Indeed, it's a new line for them, as Aleph Objects has mostly stuck with the Taz line until now. Which is important, because while there's a bunch of knowledge out there for the Taz line, the Mini has some cool new features like the auto-leveling bed, and it has a new form factor, different motherboard, and all the little things that make it harder for people to use the information that already exists. So I figured I would document what I learn here.</p>
<p>I'm also using Simplify3D for my slicing, which means transferring all the existing profiles for <a href="https://www.lulzbot.com/cura">Cura</a> into <a href="https://www.simplify3d.com">Simplify3D</a> and learning what sorts of cool things I can do with that for a better slicing experience. As I create those profiles, I'll go ahead and put them in a convenient location on the site. Hopefully I can give people a better idea of if Simplify3D is worth the $150 (it is for me, and probably is for you) as there's no free trial, so it's a big leap to go from the Free/Open Source tools to something like that.</p>
<p>Of course, when I make 3D models, I will share them on various sites and talk about them here.</p>
<p>Finally, as of when I purchased, just about the only videos that I could find for the LulzBot Mini were printing out the <a href="https://www.lulzbot.com/rocktopus">Rocktopus</a>, so I think it would be worthwhile adding a bunch of videos as well. </p>
<p>So welcome again, and let's see what sorts of trouble we can get into with 3D Printing, shall we?</p>
