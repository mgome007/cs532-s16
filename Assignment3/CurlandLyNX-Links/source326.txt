your brand.&#8221;</span></h2>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Accordingly, our business&#8217;s trademark symbol
(&#8482;)&nbsp;&nbsp; and logo are valuable &#8220;intellectual
property assets&#8221; that are protected by trademark law.&nbsp; This trademark identifies your services.&nbsp; Therefore, in order to protect your trademark
under federal law, you must file an application with the U.S. Patent and
Trademark Office (</span><span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , &quot;sans-serif&quot;; font-size: 11.0pt; line-height: 115%;"><a href="http://www.uspto.gov/"><span style="color: black; font-size: 12.0pt; line-height: 115%;">USPTO</span></a></span><span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">).</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Once you complete the task of registering your
trademark with the USPTO, you can sue others for trademark infringement.&nbsp; You will also be able to use the &#174; symbol to
protect your mark.&nbsp; However, if you do
not get your federal registration, you can still establish common law rights by
using the &#8482; symbol after the &#174; mark.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">PM Legal also notes, &#8220;That there is a difference
between trademark law protection for your company name and the act of filing
documents in a state to form your company or registering with the intent to do
business as a foreign corporation in another state.&nbsp; Filing documents with the states to reserve
your corporate name and form the business does not give you any trademark
protection, rights or ownership in your company&#8217;s name.&#8221;</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<h3 style="text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><i>Are you using Government Logo&#8217;s properly?</i></span></h3>
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">If you are using a government logo, keep in mind
that you cannot use a federal government logo without their permission.&nbsp; Check your contract to see if it describes
the best practices for displaying their crest or logo on your website.&nbsp; The agency may provide permission in the
contract.&nbsp; In addition, depending on the
agency, some agencies allow it by virtue of a blanket rule in their
regulations.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">If you are unable to find any reference to using
their logo or crest on your website, then email the agency and ask for
permission to use their logo on your site.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<h3 style="text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><i>Do you use a copyright notice on your website?</i></span></h3>
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Works of authorship: original content,
photographs, or artistic works are another type of intellectual property that
you can protect with copyright laws.&nbsp; &#8220;Certain&#8221;
elements fall within the copyright protection such as:</span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<h3 style="margin-left: .25in;">
<ul>
<li><span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; font-weight: normal; line-height: 115%;">Blog post</span></li>
<li><span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; font-weight: normal; line-height: 115%;">Marketing videos</span></li>
<li><span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; font-weight: normal; line-height: 115%;">Creating graphic elements</span></li>
</ul>
</h3>
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">The best practice to protect written work, use a
copyright notice on the bottom of your website.&nbsp;
Your copyright notice should consist of using three elements:</span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<h3 style="margin-left: 0.25in; text-align: left;">
<ol>
<li><span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; font-weight: normal; line-height: 115%;">The &#169; symbol, or the work &#8220;Copyright&#8221; or the abbreviation &#8220;copr.&#8221;</span></li>
<li><span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; font-weight: normal; line-height: 115%;">The year of the first publication</span></li>
<li><span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; font-weight: normal; line-height: 115%;">The name of the owner of copyright in the work</span></li>
</ol>
</h3>
<h4 style="text-align: left;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><i>Have you registered your domain name?</i></span></h4>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">The first step in securing your brand name, this
process is a part of your website address.&nbsp;
Best practice is registering your domain name with an accredited domain
name registrar.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">However, this is not the same as trademark
registration with the USPTO.&nbsp; &#8220;Domain
name registration does not grant trademark law protection, rights or
ownership.&#8221; ~ PM Legal</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>
<h4 style="text-align: left;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><i>Protecting Tour Trade Secrets</i></span></h4>
</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">As PM Legal explains, &#8220;your trade secrets
consist of your &#8216;secret sauce&#8217; such as financial, business, scientific, or
technical information and know-how that are unique and developed by your
company for its own proprietary use.&#8221;</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">You should take steps to protect your trade
secrets.&nbsp; Such as, monitoring your
website regularly to make sure your secrets are intact.&nbsp; Trade secrets can last forever.&nbsp; Protecting them does not require filing with
the USPTO.&nbsp; However, federally protected
intellectual property, patents and trademarks, have a defined term.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">PM Legal also advises us not to disclose any
information related to your trade secrets through marketing efforts.&nbsp; This may be tempting for gaining an advantage
over competitors when promoting your products or services.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">However, do not let your marketing efforts get
in the way of protecting your intellectual property because you may
inadvertently share some valuable information that can be damaging to the
growth of your business.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;"><br /></span></div>

<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-KzeuKeEhmQY/VPeGlgZu4eI/AAAAAAAABeM/j180Z3qUQ-k/s1600/Google-New-Mobile-Ranking-Signal-800x450.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="225" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-KzeuKeEhmQY/VPeGlgZu4eI/AAAAAAAABeM/j180Z3qUQ-k/s1600/Google-New-Mobile-Ranking-Signal-800x450.jpg" width="400" /></a></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
Google will be releasing a new &#8220;mobile-friendliness&#8221; ranking signal on April 21 and Google states this will have a &#8220;significant impact&#8221; on the search results.  The change will affect mobile searches in all languages worldwide.  Google states on their Webmaster Central Blog that users will find it easier to get relevant, high quality search results that are optimized for their devices.</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
It is well known that Google has been insisting that site owners adapt to a responsive design.  The way Google sees it:</div>
<blockquote class="tr_bq" style="text-align: justify;">
When it comes to search on mobile devices, users should get the most relevant and timely results, no matter if the information lives on mobile-friendly web pages or apps. As more people use mobile devices to access the internet, our algorithms have to adapt to these usage patterns. In the past, we&#8217;ve made updates to ensure a site is configured properly and viewed on modern devices. We&#8217;ve made it easier for users to find mobile-friendly web pages and we&#8217;ve introduced App Indexing to surface useful content from apps."</blockquote>
<h2 style="text-align: center;">
<b>Mobile-Friendly Ranking Factor</b></h2>
<br />
<div style="text-align: justify;">
On October 14, 2015, Google started displaying a gray mobile-friendly label on the mobile search results list indicating that a site is meeting their standards.  For websites that aren&#8217;t mobile-friendly they are displaying a strike-through on the mobile icon to discourage visitors.  Google is always seeking new ways to enhance their user&#8217;s experience as they browse for things on the Internet.<br />
<script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js"></script>
<!-- BTF_article_responsive -->
<br />
<ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-6432345606457765" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="8162899258" style="display: block;"></ins><script>
(adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
</script></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
To help website owners adapt to the new changes, Google provides a <a href="https://developers.google.com/webmasters/mobile-sites/get-started/?utm_source=wmc-blog&amp;utm_medium=referral&amp;utm_campaign=mobile-friendly" target="_blank"><span style="color: #b45f06;">guide for mobile-friendly</span></a> sites.  Webmasters can get ready for this change by using the following tools to see how Googlebot views your page:</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-family: &quot;Calibri&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;; font-size: 12.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-US; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-bidi; mso-fareast-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; mso-fareast-language: EN-US; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;">If
you want to test a few pages, you can use the </span><span style="font-family: &quot;Calibri&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-US; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; mso-bidi-font-size: 10.0pt; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-bidi; mso-fareast-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; mso-fareast-language: EN-US; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;"><a href="https://www.google.com/webmasters/tools/mobile-friendly/?utm_source=wmc-blog&amp;utm_medium=referral&amp;utm_campaign=mobile-friendly" rel="nofollow" target="_blank"><span style="color: #b45f06; font-size: 12.0pt;">Mobile-Friendly Test</span></a></span><span style="font-family: &quot;Calibri&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;; font-size: 12.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-US; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-bidi; mso-fareast-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; mso-fareast-language: EN-US; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;">.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-family: &quot;Calibri&quot;,&quot;sans-serif&quot;; font-size: 12.0pt; mso-ansi-language: EN-US; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-bidi; mso-fareast-font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; mso-fareast-language: EN-US; mso-fareast-theme-font: minor-fareast; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;"><span style="font-size: 12pt;">If
you already have a website, you can use your Webmaster Tools account to get a
full list of mobile usability issues across your site using the Mobile
Usability Report.</span></span></li>
</ul>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
For those who have been taking advantage of their App Index, Google will now surface content from indexed apps more prominently in search.  Google has already begun to use information from indexing apps as a factor in ranking for signed-in users who have the app installed.  If you don&#8217;t know how to use App Indexing, Google provides a <a href="https://developers.google.com/app-indexing/webmasters/details?utm_source=wmc-blog&amp;utm_medium=referral&amp;utm_campaign=mobile-friendly" target="_blank"><span style="color: #b45f06;">step-by-step guide</span></a> on their developer site.&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
Google has laid out some &#8220;criteria&#8221; for meeting their standards.  Webmasters should avoid software that isn&#8217;t common on mobile devices.  Such as Flash, Google started showing messages in mobile search indicating that a site &#8220;Uses Flash. May not work on your device.&#8221;&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
Google also states that websites should use text that is readable without zooming, and should size content to the screen so that users don&#8217;t have to scroll horizontally or zoom.  In addition, links should be placed far enough apart so that the correct one can be tapped easily.&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
Other corrections that may be necessary for your site to rank well with Google can be further gained from using their <a href="https://developers.google.com/speed/pagespeed/insights/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank"><span style="color: #b45f06;">PageSpeed Insights</span></a>  tool.</div>
