<div style="text-align: center;">
<b><span style="color: black; font-size: large;">Introduction to the Book</span></b></div>
</div>
<br />
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;">It&#8217;s wintertime in Rankins, so&#8230;</span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;">Let&#8217;s celebrate! I know, I know, you&#8217;ve had enough of winter, right? Those of you who live in the eastern half of the continent or some other snowy place have probably quit reading already. But on the off-chance that you&#8217;re still with me, let&#8217;s talk about it. I&#8217;ll start with my own confession: I don&#8217;t really like winter. Truth be known, I&#8217;m a bit of sunshine junkie. I don&#8217;t like to lay in the sun. I&#8217;m more of a hike on a sunny day, working outdoors, or hanging out by the water kind of girl. You know &#8211; a sun in the shade kind of person.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;">So after writing about every season that Alaska has to offer, I was nervous to set a book entirely during the winter. (<i>Mountains Apart</i>&nbsp;also took place in the winter, but the heroine in that book, Emily, certainly didn&#8217;t embrace it. She hated it. Of course, she didn&#8217;t like Rankins either when she first got there.) To me, winter is such a huge part of Alaska&#8217;s image. I was afraid I wouldn&#8217;t be able to do it justice.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;">I thought about my own strategy for surviving winter. Escaping to Hawaii being cost prohibitive, I opt to get out and do something in spite of the weather. For me, this means bundling up and taking a hike, or, if I&#8217;m really lucky, skiing. Obviously, this isn&#8217;t something I can do every day, but it gives me something to look forward to during the often-dreary days of winter. It&#8217;s a way to face it, to embrace the cold and turn it into something fun. That&#8217;s when it hit me. That&#8217;s exactly what Alaskans do. They take the cold, the snow, the ice and the dark and use it to their advantage. And in some of the most incredibly creative ways (more about this in a future blog). That&#8217;s the route I opted for in&nbsp;<i>A Family Like Hannah&#8217;s</i>.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;">Hannah and Tate embrace the winter &#8211; she&#8217;s a skier and he&#8217;s a snowboarder. I tried to put them outdoors as much as I could &#8211; skiing, snowboarding, sledding, snowshoeing. There&#8217;s even a bonfire, and plenty of hot chocolate. And always, at the forefront of the story, is Snowy Sky Resort &#8211; the business whose future they are battling over.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;"><i>A Family Like Hannah&#8217;s</i>&nbsp;is my version of an Alaskan winter - and more specifically - a wintertime in Rankins. On each stop of the tour, I&#8217;ve tried to infuse some of this enduring and inspiring winter spirit Alaskans seem to so easily embrace. Hope you enjoy!</span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<span style="color: black;">&#8212; Carol</span></div>

<div style="text-align: start;">
<div>
<div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<b>(Hearts in Autumn #2)</b></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<b>by Joyce DePastena</b></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Adult Historical Romance</div>
</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
ebook, 348 pages</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
February 17th 2016</div>
</div>
<div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/28671080-courting-cassandry" target="_blank"><img src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-YGVAvMLd0pk/Uw-sPzUtPII/AAAAAAAAD38/gWPHjTZWAh8/s1600/Add+to+Goodreads+Button.png" /></a></div>
</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Is it too late for second chances when the girl you loved in your youth comes back into your life?</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Gerolt de Warenne became guardian to a child-heiress named Cassandry when he was only nineteen-years old. As he watched her grow into a lovely young woman, he found himself falling in love with her, but Cassandry viewed him as an older brother. So, burying his feelings, he gave permission for her to marry another.</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Twenty-four years later Gerolt and Cassandry meet again. With the loss of their respective spouses, Gerolt hopes to finally court Cassandry, but she desires to remain a widow. Instead, they agree to a betrothal of their children.</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Matters become complicated as their friendship begins to evolve into the romance Gerolt has always wanted. But by the law of the medieval Church, Cassandry and Gerolt can&#8217;t marry if their children do. Can they find a way to be together? Or must they sacrifice their future for the love of their children?</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
"Courting Cassandry" is a &#8220;Hearts in Autumn&#8221; romance, medieval romances revolving around heroes and heroines &#8220;in the autumn of their years.&#8221; Because love isn&#8217;t only for the young!</div>
<div>
<div>

</div>
</div>
<div>
<div>
<div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<b>(Hearts in Autumn #1)</b></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<b>by Joyce DePastena</b></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Adult Historical Romance</div>
</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Paperback &amp; ebook, 220 pages</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
October 11th 2014</div>
</div>
<div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/23379271-loving-lucianna" target="_blank"><img src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-YGVAvMLd0pk/Uw-sPzUtPII/AAAAAAAAD38/gWPHjTZWAh8/s1600/Add+to+Goodreads+Button.png" /></a></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Sir Balduin de Soler gave up long ago on love. He never had the means to support a wife until an unexpected advancement in his fifties allows him to reassess his future just as the lovely Lucianna enters his life.&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Lucianna Fabio harbors a secret, painful memory from her past that has kept her unwed, as well. Now in her forties, she thought herself too old to marry until she meets Sir Balduin. Now suddenly their lonely autumn lives feel very much like spring again . . . until Lucianna&#8217;s brother appears without warning and threatens to revive the secret that will destroy Lucianna&#8217;s second chance at love.&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
"Loving Lucianna" is the first in Joyce DiPastena&#8217;s new "Hearts in Autumn" romance series, medieval romances revolving around heroes and heroines &#8220;in the autumn of their years.&#8221; Because you&#8217;re never too old to fall in love!</div>

The university was also where she completed her first full-length novel&#8230;set, of course, in medieval England. Later, her fascination with Henry II led her to expand her research horizons to the far reaches of his &#8220;Angevin Empire&#8221; in France, which became the setting of her first published novel, Loyalty&#8217;s Web (a 2007 Whitney Award Finalist).</div>

<div style="text-align: left;">
When Callie Vinerelli gives refuge in her Cape Cod home to a storm ravaged stranger, the hidden secres of her house on the dunes begin to emerge. She becomes enmeshed in the violence and tragedy of the past that left accomplished artist Christina Burroughs a paraplegic and stole her beloved daughter from her. Those still unresolved events invade Callie's life, causing chaos and intrigue, life changing relationships, justice and peace to Christina's descendants -and new love to herself.</div>

<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
In a ramshackle old cottage with an outside lav, and a rickety ladder up to a draughty attic bedroom, Jim and Mary Norris slog in all weathers to make a living from their market garden, and to bring up their little girl, Patsy. But at times Jim&#8217;s guilt is overwhelming as he watches his beloved Mary working her frozen fingers to the bone, almost dropping with weariness &#8211; not exactly what she&#8217;s been used to in her genteel upbringing as the niece of well- off local gentry, who cut her off when she married &#8220;beneath her&#8221;. But she never complains; she loves Jim madly and they are blissfully happy together.</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Until her accident &#8211; and the nightmare that follows them all through the next 30 years. Mary&#8217;s gradual descent into madness overshadows even Jim&#8217;s second marriage and children, but the shocking climax is something no-one could have foreseen.</div>

<b><span style="font-size: large;">Janet Purcell</span></b>&nbsp;is a professional artist and writes a weekly fine arts column and feature articles for&nbsp;<i>The Times of Trenton</i>&nbsp;(NJ). Her interior design features appear regularly in&nbsp;<i>DesignNJ</i>&nbsp;magazine. Her articles have appeared in&nbsp;<i>Woman&#8217;s Day</i>&nbsp;Special Interest Publications,&nbsp;<i>Bridal Guide</i>,&nbsp;<i>American Spa</i>, and many other magazines and travel guides. Her travel stories have frequently been published in the&nbsp;<i>New York Post</i>,&nbsp;<i>Country Discoveries</i>, and in Princeton, New Jersey&#8217;s&nbsp;<i>Packet Publications</i>&#8217; community newspapers. Her previous novel,&nbsp;<i>Singer Lane</i>, was published in 2008 and her third novel, Rooster Street, is awaiting publication. Though not a trilogy, many of the same characters are found continuing their lives in each of the three books. Her paintings can be seen at&nbsp;<a href="http://www.janpurcellart.com/">www.janpurcellart.com</a>.</div>
